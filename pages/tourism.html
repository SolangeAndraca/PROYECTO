Open-air cinemas in Berlin

Open-air cinema season in Berlin runs from May to September. They offer a varied film program from cult films to the latest Hollywood films in the open air. There is a different film every day this year, including highlights of the current cinema season, as well as classics and cult films. The open-air cinema in Berlin's has been digitized for several seasons and thus promises visitors a significantly improved viewing experience. As a rule, all films are shown in the original version with subtitles. A different film is shown every day, with German films showing English subtitles. Dogs may be brought on a leash.

Museums

Discover current exhibitions and the highlights of the collections! Berlin's Museum Island with its five museums is the largest museum ensemble in the world and is listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Other museums, top exhibitions and contemporary art galleries throughout Berlin await your visit. Find even more thingst to do in our event calendar. The first Sunday of the month is free of charge at around 60 Berlin museums! These include all the buildings on Berlin's Museum Island, the Humboldt Forum, Museum für Naturkunde, Jewish Museum, Gemäldegalerie and many more.

Sights

Charlottenburg Palace: is located just out of the centre of the city. It hosts fine collections of China and paintings. The Berlin Wall Memorial: is located between the districts of Wedding and Mitte on Bernauer Straße. Victory Column: is one of the most famous landmarks of the German capital. It was erected in the 19th century and originally commemorated Germany's wars of unity. Today it has become a symbol of peace. Kurfürstendamm: is the most expensive address in the capital city and home to the most exclusive brands. Gendarmenmarkt: s one of the most stunning squares in the city, located close to Friedrichstraße.

City Tours

Discover Berlin by booking a guided tour through the city. Experienced city guides show and explain sights and monuments in Berlin, lead you to the remnants of the Berlin Wall and through neighbourhoods. Some focus on history, art and culture. Other guides offer to explore the city on foot, by bike or at a shopping tour. The tour includes a visit to Brandenburg Gate and the famous boulevards Unter den Linden and Kurfürstendamm. It will also take you to Berlin’s Government district with the Reichstag and Federal Chancellery. There are also tours on specific topics, such as the Cold War and the Berlin Wall, the Third Reich and World War II.

Events this Month

Rave the Planet Under the motto "Music is the Answer," the participants are standing up for love, peace, friendship, solidarity, diversity, respect, music, sustainability and community. The organizers' demands include the recognition of electronic music culture as a cultural asset to be protected, the abolition of dance bans and the protection of cultural venues such as clubs.
The Lesbian and Gay Festival in Berlin is Europe's largest street festival of its kind. It takes place in Nollendorfplatz, the traditional gay stomping ground.
On Christopher Street Day hundreds of thousands of people take to the streets to fight for the rights of LGBTQ people.

Flea Markets

Whether you are looking for a great bargain or a unique gift, Berlin's flea markets offer a relaxed shopping experience and a great activity for your Saturday or Sunday afternoon. Browse through an endless range of items from vintage furniture and clothes to records and antiques and enjoy the pleasure of making a great bargain. Trying some of the tasty foods and drinks offered at the markets is a must-do. The markets itself consists of mainly private vendors selling various items such as second-hand clothes, shoes, bicycles, old vinyl records, jewelry and other various knickknacks.

Parks

With more than 2,500 public parks, covering a total-surface of approx. 6,500 ha, Berlin´s parks are an integral part of the city’s culture, many home to intriguing historical monuments, opportunities for outdoor activities and quintessential German beer gardens. In addition to well-known names such as Tiergarten and Tempelhofer Feld, among Berlin’s most spectacular green spaces are the lush Grunewald Forest, historic Volkspark Friedrichshain and the gardens of Charlottenburg Palace. If you want to go green, Berlin is the place to do it. Biking, skateboarding and barbecuing are not permitted in principle
